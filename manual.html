Introduction

TANGUY creates sounds in real time from data input through the user interface ("the panel"). Skillful synthesizer programming can yield a wide variety of sounds including percussive metallic banging, electronic beeps, bizarre effects, emulations of acoustic instruments, and warm "analog" tones.

System Requirements

TANGUY sounds best in Google Chrome, Opera, or Apple Safari. Firefox has some known issues with frequency modulation that prevent Oscillator Two from making sounds. MIDI implementations will require Google Chrome.

Controls

The presets included in TANGUY will give you a quick impression of its capabilities.

Choosing a Preset

Click on any part of the field that contains the text INITIALIZE to open the list of presets. Select any preset to load it or click outside of the list to cancel. Some presets have VCA Gain greater than 0, and will begin making sound automatically.

Playing the Keyboard

TANGUY is a monosynth: only one note can be played from the keyboard at a time. Click the keys with your mouse or play TANGUY using your QWERTY keyboard. The home row represents the white keys, and the black keys are mapped to the corresponding keys in the row above.

Quickly shift the range of the keyboard up or down using the buttons -1 and +1 buttons or the keyboard shortcuts: - and = .

The Pitch slider offers real time performance opportunities for bending the pitch. Move the slider to shift the pitch of all tracked oscillators simultaneously. Release to allow the pitch to snap back to its original value. Pitch bend and snapping do not work in Firefox.

Dynamically change the amount of the LFO's effects by using the Mod. slider. If Mod. is set to 0, the LFO will have no effect. Since the Mod. wheel determines the amount of the LFO, it is considered part of the preset.

Programming Sounds

Delay

Reverb effects can be achieved by setting a very short delay with an amount at roughly 50%.

An overdrive (not really overdrive) effect can be achieved by setting delay to the lowest possible setting and amount above 50% (probably happens at a lower percentage -- test).

Troubleshooting

If TANGUY is not producing sound, check filter cutoff, filter mode, mixer levels, and amp envelope settings.
